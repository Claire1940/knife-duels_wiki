<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Hitbox Mastery">

The technical side of **knife duels 1v1** involves understanding "active frames." When you trigger an attack, there is a wind-up, an active period where the knife can deal damage, and a recovery phase. Mastering the 2026 combat system requires you to internalize these phases so you never leave yourself vulnerable.

  <Card title="The Wind-Up">

  <Card title="Active Frames">
    - **The damage window**
    - Requires precise aiming

  <Card title="Recovery Phase">
    - **Most vulnerable state**

  </Card>

In the current meta, players often use "dragging" techniques. By moving your camera in the direction of your swing, you can technically extend the reach or duration of your active frames, catching opponents who think they have already dodged the strike.

| Attack Type | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quick Slash** | Fast recovery, hard to punish | Lower damage, short reach |
| **Heavy Stab** | High damage, long reach | Long wind-up, easy to dodge |
| **Jump Attack** | Confuses camera, bypasses blocks | Predictable arc, high risk |
| **Feint** | Forces early enemy swings | Consumes stamina/time |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Avoid "spam clicking." Most knife duel engines penalize rapid, mindless attacking by increasing the recovery time between swings, making you an easy target for a disciplined player.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced 1v1 Strategies and Tactics">

Once you understand the mechanics, you must develop a tactical mindset. A **knife duels 1v1** is often won before the first strike is even landed. Psychological warfare, such as "conditioning" your opponent to expect one type of movement before switching to another, is what separates elite players from the rest of the community.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Observation Phase">
    Spend the first 10-15 seconds of the duel observing. Does your opponent rush? Do they wait for you to move first? Identifying their "aggro" level is crucial.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Bait and Switch">
    Move into the opponent's range and immediately back out. If they swing and miss, they enter the recovery phase, giving you a free window to strike.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Finishing Blow">
    Once you land a hit, don't get greedy. Reset your spacing or commit to a combo only if you are certain the opponent is staggered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Strategy | Execution | Best Against |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Passive-Aggressive** | Wait for misses, then punish | Aggressive rushers |
| **Circle Strafing** | Constant lateral movement | Players with low sensitivity |
| **The Wall Pin** | Forcing the enemy into a corner | Defensive/Passive players |
| **Jump Baiting** | Jumping to draw an upward swing | Sturdy, grounded opponents |

<Callout type="success" title="Winning Strategy">
If you find yourself against a much faster opponent, try to minimize your own movement. Force them to come to you, reducing the variables you need to track and allowing you to focus entirely on timing your counter-swing.
</Callout>

</Section>
