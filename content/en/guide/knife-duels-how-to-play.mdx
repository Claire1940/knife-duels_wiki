<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Combat Mechanics and Hitboxes">

The heart of **knife duels how to play** lies in the interaction between your weapon and the enemy's hitbox. The game features a unique physics engine where thrown knives have travel time and drop, requiring players to "lead" their shots. Stabbing, on the other hand, requires close proximity but offers a faster execution if the enemy is caught off-guard.

One of the most discussed features in the community is the **Aim Assist**. For beginners, this mechanic helps lock onto targets, making throws more consistent. However, experienced players often find that the lock-on can be restrictive when trying to predict an opponent's erratic movement.

| Attack Type | Range | Damage Potential | Best Usage |
|-------------|-------|------------------|------------|
| **Stab** | Very Short | High / Instant | When an opponent misses a throw and is reloading |
| **Quick Throw** | Medium | Moderate | Rapid fire to pressure an opponent's movement |
| **Charged Throw** | Long | High / Headshot | Sniping opponents from across the map or behind cover |

<Callout type="warning" title="Hitbox Awareness">
The hitboxes for stabbing can occasionally feel inconsistent due to server latency. If you are lagging, prioritize throwing knives over melee combat to avoid "ghost stabs" where your blade appears to hit but registers no damage.
</Callout>

### Understanding the Aim Assist System
The Aim Assist in Knife Duels is notably strong compared to other Roblox dueling games. It provides a "magnetic" pull toward the opponent's torso or head. While this is excellent for learning the ropes, mastering the game involves knowing when to toggle it off for more creative projectile paths.

| Setting | Effect on Gameplay | Recommended For |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------|
| **Aim Assist ON** | Locks cursor near targets | Beginners / Mobile Players |
| **Aim Assist OFF** | Full manual control of arc | Advanced / Competitive Players |
| **High Sensitivity** | Faster 180-degree turns | Aggressive "Rush" playstyles |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Winning Matches">

Winning a match consistently requires more than just good aim; it requires tactical positioning and resource management. Follow these steps to improve your win rate in the 2026 meta.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze Opponent Movement">
    Spend the first round observing if your opponent jumps frequently or strafes in a predictable pattern. This tells you where to aim your first throw.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Control the High Ground">
    Most maps feature verticality. Standing on crates or ledges makes it harder for opponents to hit your head while giving you a better angle for downward throws.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Your Health">
    If you take a hit, do not panic. Use the environment to break line of sight. Since you heal automatically, a 5-second retreat can reset the fight in your favor.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Bait and Stab">
    Throw a knife intentionally wide to bait the opponent into rushing you. As they close the distance, switch to melee and execute a quick stab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Map Awareness">
