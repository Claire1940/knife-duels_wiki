<Section icon={Map} title="Movement Mastery and Spacing Tactics">

Movement is 70% of the battle in **knife duels gameplay**. If you cannot control the distance between you and your enemy, you have already lost. Advanced movement techniques allow you to manipulate your hitbox, making it significantly harder for the opponent to land a clean hit.

**Key Movement Techniques:**
- **Counter-Strafing**: Tapping the opposite movement key to come to an instant halt for a precision strike.
- **Crouch-Ducking**: Lowering your profile just as the opponent swings for a headshot.
- **Circle Strafing**: Moving in a tight arc around the opponent to stay behind their field of view.

| Movement Action | Advantage | Risk Factor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint-Lunge** | Closes gap instantly | High recovery if missed |
| **Jump-Slash** | Confuses hitbox height | Predictable falling arc |
| **Backpedal** | Baits opponent forward | Can be cornered easily |
| **Side-Step** | Avoids linear thrusts | Requires perfect timing |

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Note">
Use the environment to your advantage. Narrow corridors favor thrusting weapons, while open courtyards allow for the circular movement required by high-speed slashers.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Competitive Strategies for 2026">

In the 2026 meta, **knife duels gameplay** has evolved into a high-speed chess match. Top players use "flick-baiting," where they intentionally look away or start a movement in one direction only to snap back and strike. This forces the opponent to commit to a defensive action too early.

| Strategy Name | Execution | Counter-Play |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Bait-and-Switch** | Feign a retreat, then 180-degree lunge | Maintain gap, don't chase |
| **The Turtle** | Focus entirely on parrying and counters | Use heavy attacks to break guard |
| **The Whirlwind** | Constant jumping and spinning attacks | Crouch and strike the legs |
| **The Shadow** | Staying directly behind the opponent | High-sensitivity 180-turns |

Psychological warfare is also a major factor. If you consistently win the first two rounds of a duel, the opponent will often become overly aggressive or overly defensive. Recognizing this shift in their "mental state" allows you to adjust your tactics accordingly.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
If an opponent is "turtling" (playing purely defensive), try to use "ghost swings"—striking the air just in front of them to bait out their parry, then punishing them while their parry is on cooldown.
</Callout>
