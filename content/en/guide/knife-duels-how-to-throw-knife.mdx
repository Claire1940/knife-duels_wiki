<Section icon={Target} title="Knife Duels how to throw knife: Step-by-Step Guide">

Throwing your knife is one of the most satisfying yet difficult skills to master in the game. It requires a combination of projectile arc prediction and enemy movement reading. If you miss a throw, you may be left temporarily unarmed or forced to wait for a cooldown, depending on the specific game mode rules.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip a Throwable Blade">
    Ensure your current knife skin or weapon type supports throwing. Most standard knives in the current 2026 meta allow for a secondary throw action.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Calculate the Arc">
    Knives in this game do not travel in a perfectly straight line over long distances. Aim slightly above your target's head if they are far away to account for gravity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Predict Enemy Movement">
    Do not throw where the enemy is; throw where they are going to be. If an opponent is strafing left, lead your shot to the left of their current position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Throw">
    Use the designated keybind (usually Right Mouse Button or a specific UI button on Mobile). Release the input when your crosshair aligns with the predicted path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Follow Up or Recover">
    If the knife hits, it often results in an instant elimination or massive damage. If you miss, immediately switch to defensive movement to avoid being punished.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Throwing your knife makes you extremely vulnerable. Only attempt a throw when you are certain of the hit or have enough distance to recover if you miss.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Advanced Strategies for Winning Duels">

Once you understand **knife duels how to throw knife** and basic movement, you must develop a tactical mindset. High-level play is less about clicking fast and more about psychological warfare.

### Baiting and Punishing
The most common mistake beginners make is running directly at their opponent while swinging. Instead, walk into the edge of their range and immediately backpedal. This often "baits" the opponent into swinging and missing. Once they are in their recovery frames, lung forward for a guaranteed hit.

### The "Circle Strafe" Technique
By holding the "A" or "D" key while moving forward, you move in an arc. This makes it significantly harder for opponents to land a knife throw on you. In melee combat, circle-strafing allows you to get behind the opponent's guard, where they cannot block or effectively counter-attack.

### Spatial Awareness
In Free-For-All (FFA) modes, never stay in the center of the arena. Position yourself near the edges so you can monitor the entire field. This prevents you from being "sandwiched" between two other players fighting.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Highlight">
Watch your opponent's feet. Their movement direction usually dictates where they will strike. If they stop moving suddenly, they are likely preparing for a block or a precision throw.
</Callout>
