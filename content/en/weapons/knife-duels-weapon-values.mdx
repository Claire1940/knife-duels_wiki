### S-Tier Weapons

The S-Tier weapons represent the absolute peak of the current meta. These blades offer minimal recovery frames, allowing players to execute rapid follow-up strikes or transition instantly into a defensive block.

The **Katana** stands out due to its exceptional range. In a game where spacing is everything, the ability to land a lethal strike from outside your opponent's threat radius is invaluable. Meanwhile, the **Butterfly Knife** offers unparalleled mobility and fluid animations, making it the preferred choice for aggressive players who rely on rapid strafing and erratic movement patterns to confuse their targets.

### A-Tier Weapons

A-Tier weapons are incredibly viable and can easily challenge S-Tier options when utilized by a skilled combatant. These weapons typically excel in one specific area but possess minor drawbacks that prevent them from achieving absolute dominance.

The **Dagger** boasts the fastest raw swing speed in the game, making it deadly in face-to-face scrambles. However, its extremely short reach requires you to get dangerously close to your opponent. Conversely, the **Machete** provides excellent sweeping coverage and solid range, but its slightly slower recovery speed leaves a narrow window for counter-attacks if you miss your initial swing.

### B-Tier and C-Tier Weapons

Weapons in these lower categories are generally outclassed by their higher-tier counterparts. The **Cleaver** deals heavy, satisfying blows, but its slow swing speed makes it highly predictable and easy to block. The **Basic Knife** serves as your starting weapon; while balanced, it lacks any specialized advantages in speed or reach, making an upgrade your top priority.

Succeeding in high-stakes duels requires more than just rapid clicking. You must adopt a systematic approach to each encounter, analyzing your opponent's habits and exploiting their mechanical errors. Follow this step-by-step combat sequence to consistently outmaneuver your rivals.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Assess the Arena and Matchup">
    Immediately identify your opponent's equipped weapon. Note their maximum potential range and swing speed relative to your own blade. Establish a mental boundary of their threat zone before engaging.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bait the Initial Swing">
    Approach the edge of the opponent's range, then quickly sprint backward (using Shift on PC). This movement trick often baits inexperienced players into swinging early, forcing them into a vulnerable recovery animation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute the Whiff Punish">
    The instant you see the opponent's swing miss, close the distance immediately. Strike during their recovery frames when they are mechanically unable to block or initiate a counter-swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Integrate Active Blocking">
    If the opponent refuses to swing first, initiate a controlled approach. Prepare to press your block key (Ctrl on PC) the moment they raise their arm, then immediately follow up with a counter-attack.
  </Step>
</Steps>
