<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Combat: Stabbing vs. Throwing">

Success in a duel depends on your ability to alternate between close-quarters stabbing and long-range throwing. While stabbing offers immediate lethality if you can close the gap, throwing allows for defensive play and "sitting back" to pick off aggressive opponents. Understanding the nuances of these two modes is essential for climbing the leaderboard.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Stabbing Strategy">
    - **Close Range**: Best for punishing players who miss their throws.
    - **Movement**: Requires erratic strafing to avoid being hit while approaching.
    - **Risk**: High risk of being counter-stabbed due to current hitbox limitations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Throwing Strategy">
    - **Long Range**: Ideal for maintaining distance and controlling the map.
    - **Precision**: Highly dependent on aim assist or manual leading of targets.
    - **Headshots**: Offers a higher satisfaction and faster kill time.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Section icon={Target} title="Technical Optimization and Settings">

To perform at your best, you must configure your settings to handle the fast-paced nature of the game. This includes managing aim assist and mitigating lag, which can be a significant hurdle during peak hours. The **Knife Duels Discord** frequently hosts discussions on the "best" settings for different hardware configurations.

**Key Technical Considerations:**
- **Aim Assist**: This feature locks onto targets, making throwing much easier. However, some top-tier players prefer turning it off for more granular control.
- **Healing Mechanic**: Unlike some hardcore shooters, you do heal over time in this game. Knowing when to retreat and recover is vital.
- **Lag Management**: High latency can make stabbing nearly impossible. Always try to play on servers closest to your physical location.

| Setting | Recommendation | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aim Assist** | ON (Beginners) | Compensates for fast movement |
| **Aim Assist** | OFF (Pros) | Allows for leading shots manually |
| **Graphics** | Low/Medium | Ensures stable FPS during 2v2s |
| **FOV** | High | Better peripheral vision for flankers |
